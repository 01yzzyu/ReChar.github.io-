Abstract

AI-Generated Content (AIGC) has recently surged in popularity, driven by its unparalleled efficiency, consistent production quality, and exceptional capacity for customization and diversification. While applications of image generation extend across numerous domains, research into stylised language writing system generation remains limited. Inspired by the pictogram subset of Chinese characters, our proposed approach, ReChar, aims to create novel artworks that seamlessly integrate text-guided decorative elements and stylistic preferences within character structures. This approach not only enhances aesthetic appeals but also opens new avenues for cultural and artistic expression. We evaluate ReChar on Chinese, Japanese, English, and Latin characters, demonstrating that the generated characters preserve their original structures while embedding decorative elements into the strokes and incorporating user-defined styles. Comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods shows that ReChar consistently outperforms these approaches.

Method

Our ReChar Framework. ReChar leverages CLIP encoder for semantic alignment and employs the cross-attention mechanism for feature integration, generating stylized character images that preserve the essence of the original content while embodying the artistic attributes of the reference style and incorporating text-guided elements.In order to provide a clear illustration of our pipeline, we will examine the generation process of an instance as a case study.
